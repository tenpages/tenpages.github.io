Group and Population Based Social Network Privacy Attacks
Master thesis, Georgetown University
Advisor: Lisa Singh (Professor, Georgetown University)

I am proposing possible methods for inferencing attribute values of social network accounts based on populated information from target accounts' groups or communities on social networks. I will analyze efficiencies of proposed methods, and possibly will propose methods to prevent such kind of attacks.
This research is for my master thesis. We also plan to submit the current progress to a conference in the upcoming Spring.

Estimation of Sparse Graph with Lifecycle
Undergraduate thesis, Georgetown University
Advisor: Yun Xiong (Professor, Fudan University), Xiangnan Kong (Associate Professor, WPI)

I proposed a new kind of sparse graph estimation problem, based on domain knowledge of periods of node activities, namely lifecycle, then addressed the estimation problem with altered pathway graphical lasso algorithm.
This research was my undergraduate thesis. It is also a part of a project in the lab of Prof. Xiong's.
